agent-slack is a free, open-source CLI for reading Slack from your terminal. By
installing the Slack app or using the CLI and its hosted OAuth relay, you agree
to these terms.

## The service

The service consists of the agent-slack CLI, which runs entirely on your
machine, and a stateless OAuth relay (`aslk.vercel.app`) that forwards Slack's
login callback to your localhost. There is no other backend, and the service is
free of charge.

## Your responsibilities

Slack tokens are stored only on your machine; keeping that machine secure is
your responsibility. If you connect agent-slack to an AI agent, the Slack
content you read is passed to that agent's model provider. That is your choice
and your responsibility.

## Privacy

The project stores no Slack data on any server. See [Privacy](/docs/privacy).

## Availability and changes

The hosted relay is provided on a best-effort basis and may change or be
discontinued at any time. Login does not depend on it: the CLI supports a
localhost redirect that bypasses the relay entirely. These terms may be
updated; the current version is always at this URL.

## Termination

You can stop using the service at any time. Workspace admins can revoke the
app from Manage Apps, and operators can run `agent-slack auth logout`, which
revokes the token on Slack. Abusive use of the hosted relay may be blocked.
